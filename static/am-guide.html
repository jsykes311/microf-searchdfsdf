Section 1

Logging In

AC Reporter uses your existing Microf Microsoft account for authentication. No separate password is required — just sign in with the same credentials you use for Outlook and Teams.

  1. 1
    Open the app in your browser

    Navigate to the Render URL your team has shared (e.g., https://microf-ac.onrender.com). You can bookmark it for quick access.

  2. 2
    Click "Sign in with Microsoft"

    You'll see the login screen below with a blue button. Click it to be redirected to Microsoft's secure sign-in flow.

  3. 3
    Use your @microf.com account

    Enter your firstname.lastname@microf.com email and password. If your organization uses multi-factor authentication, complete the MFA prompt.

  4. 4
    You're in — you'll land on the Apps Grid

    After successful authentication, you're taken directly to the Apps page where you can choose what to do.

ℹ️
Microsoft SSO Access is restricted to @microf.com accounts only. If you see an "Access Denied" message, confirm you're signed into the correct Microsoft account — you may need to sign out of a personal Microsoft account first.
🔑
Missing apps or access issues? If you can log in but don't see an app you need, or you receive a permission error, email jsykes@microf.com to request an access change.

Section 3

Global Search

Global Search lets you query all data objects at once — accounts, contacts, and SLP records — from a single search box. It's the fastest way to find anything when you're not sure exactly where to look.

How to search

  1. 1
    Click Global Search from the Apps Grid

    The search box opens inline. You can search by account name (or partial name), dealer ID, contact email, oracle ID, channel name, or any keyword.

  2. 2
    Review grouped results

    Results are returned grouped by type — Accounts, Contacts, and SLP Records. Each card shows the name and key identifiers. Accounts matched via a contact's email show a via contact badge.

  3. 3
    Click a result

    Clicking navigates to the full account or contact detail in ActiveCampaign.

  4. 4
    Export to CSV

    Use the Export CSV button to download the current results as a spreadsheet.

💡
Search tip — partial names work great Searching "comfort air" will match "COMFORT AIR LLC-44821 DBA ARS of Dallas". For ARS accounts, try searching by city name or dealer ID.

Section 4

Account View

The Account View shows everything about a single account — its core fields, SLP (Strategic Lending Partner) program records, and associated contacts. You reach it by clicking any result in Global Search, or via the account lookup within the main app.

Core Account Information

At the top of the detail panel you'll see:

  • Account Name — full name including DBA (Doing Business As) if applicable
  • Account Type — Contractor, Dealer, etc.
  • Sales Region — Southeast, Mid-Atlantic, West, or Mid-West
  • Dealer ID — the unique ID used to match this account across systems
  • Account Owner — the Account Manager assigned to this account in ActiveCampaign

SLP Records Tab

Each account can have one or more SLP records — one per lending program (e.g., an account may have both a 360 Finance SLP and an OPTIMUS SLP). The table shows:

  • Channel / Platform — the lending program (360 Finance, OPTIMUS, LTO, Microf, etc.)
  • Status — current onboarding/activation status
  • Activation Date — date the contractor was activated on this program
  • Oracle IDs — the Oracle producer ID(s) associated with this record
  • Assigned BDR — the BDR responsible for this SLP
  • Doing Business In — states where this dealer operates

Contacts Tab

Switch to the Contacts tab to see all contacts linked to this account: their name, email address, phone, and role/title as stored in ActiveCampaign.

✅
Jump to ActiveCampaign Every account detail view has a "View in ActiveCampaign →" link. Click it to open the live AC record in a new tab, where you can make edits, send emails, or review activity history.

Logging Activity Notes

The Activity Notes panel in the account sidebar lets you log any interaction with a dealer directly in ActiveCampaign — without leaving AC Reporter.

  1. 1
    Open an account

    Use Global Search to find the account. The Activity Notes panel appears in the right sidebar of the account detail view.

  2. 2
    Select the note type

    Choose from Internal Note, Call, Email, or Text.

  3. 3
    Fill in the subject and body

    Add a short subject line and your note details. Notes are saved directly to the AC account record.

  4. 4
    Click Submit

    The note saves immediately and appears in the notes list above the form.

Logging Training Records

The Training Records panel lets you log a training session for a dealer directly from the account view. Records are saved to ActiveCampaign and automatically appear in the Training Summary and Training Activity reports.

  1. 1
    Select the training type

    Choose Webinar, Video Link, or In Person.

  2. 2
    Select the agenda

    Choose Enrollment Training (first-time) or Refresh Training (returning dealer).

  3. 3
    Select the trainer and date

    Pick who conducted the training. The date defaults to today — change it if logging a past session.

  4. 4
    Add notes and click Submit

    Notes are optional. Click Submit to save the record — it will appear in the training list above and show up in reports immediately.

Pre-Built Reports

The Reports library gives you one-click access to curated data exports. Each report has pre-configured fields and built-in filters. Click the Pre-Built Reports card on the Apps Grid to open the report library.

How to Run a Report

  1. 1
    Click a report card

    From the reports library, click the card for the report you want to run. Each card shows the report name and a short description.

  2. 2
    Adjust the filters

    A filter panel will appear. Set your date range, platform, region, or other filters as needed. Leave a filter blank to include all values.

  3. 3
    Click "Run Report"

    Results appear in a table below the filters. Larger reports may take a few seconds to load.

  4. 4
    Download as CSV

    Click the Download CSV button to export the results to a spreadsheet. The filename includes the report name and date.

💡
Date filters — start broad, then narrow When you're not sure of exact dates, start with a wide range (e.g., the whole quarter) and scan the results before narrowing down. All date filters use your local timezone.

Section 6

Report Builder

Report Builder lets you create custom ad-hoc queries against ActiveCampaign data. Use it when you need a combination of fields that no pre-built report covers — for example, "all accounts in the Southeast region on the LTO program with a last app date in the last 90 days."

How to Build a Custom Report

  1. 1
    Choose a data object

    Select whether you want to query Accounts, Contacts, or SLP Records. This determines which fields are available.

  2. 2
    Select the fields (columns) you want

    Check the boxes for each field you want to appear in the output. For Accounts these include name, region, dealer ID, type, owner, and any custom fields. For SLPs: platform, status, activation date, oracle IDs, and more.

  3. 3
    Add filters

    Apply one or more filter conditions to narrow results — e.g., Region = Southeast, Platform = LTO, or Status = Contractor Activated.

  4. 4
    Run and export

    Click Run Query. Results appear below. Download via Export CSV to share or analyze in Excel.

ℹ️
When to use Report Builder vs Pre-Built Reports Use pre-built reports for day-to-day tasks — they're faster and already formatted. Use Report Builder when you need an unusual combination of fields, want to cross SLP data with account fields, or are doing a one-off data pull for a specific project.

Section 7

Dealer Locator

The Dealer Locator opens at /dealer-locator in a new tab and shows an interactive map of active Microf dealers across your territory. Use it to quickly see dealer density in a region, verify coverage for a zip code, or look up a specific dealer by clicking its pin.

Using the Map

  • Green pins — Contractor Activated dealers
  • Blue pins — dealers with other active statuses
  • Orange pins — dealers on hold or with limited activity
  • Click any pin to open a summary card in the right panel showing the account name, dealer ID, program, and activation date
  • Zoom and pan using standard map controls or your scroll wheel

Side Panel — Ranked by Last App Date

The right panel lists nearby Microf-channel dealers ranked by Last App Date using a 3-tier system. The dealer with the most recent application is ranked 1st Choice, the second most recent is 2nd Choice, and all others are 3rd Choice. Within each tier, dealers are sorted by distance.

  • 1st Choice — most recently active dealer in the area
  • 2nd Choice — second most recently active dealer
  • 3rd Choice — all remaining dealers, sorted by distance
  • Dealers with no last app date on file are treated as 3rd Choice and sorted by distance
  • Click any card in the list to highlight and center its pin on the map
ℹ️
Microf dealers only The map and side panel show dealers with a Microf-channel SLP (including Microf Direct, Microf LTO, etc.) that are Contractor Activated. Non-Microf programs like OPTIMUS or 360 Finance are not shown here.
💡
Territory planning use case Zoom into a metro area — the side panel immediately shows you which dealers are actively using Microf (recent last app dates at the top) and which have gone quiet. Use this to prioritize re-engagement calls before heading into the field.
